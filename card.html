Green Sea Turtle

Green Sea Turtle is the largest hard-shelled sea turtle, reaching up to 5 ft long and 700 lbs.

  • Scientific Name: Chelonia mydas
  • Average Length: 5 ft
  • Average Lifespan: 80 years
  • Habitat: Tropical and subtropical oceans

Green sea turtles are unique among sea turtles because they are herbivores, primarily feeding on seagrasses and algae. This diet gives their fat a greenish hue, hence their name. They also exhibit extensive long-distance migrations, traveling thousands of miles between feeding grounds and nesting beaches.

Siberian Tiger

Siberian Tigers are the largest cats in the world, and the rarest tiger subspecies.

  • Scientific Name: Panthera tigris altaica
  • Average Length: 10 ft
  • Average Lifespan: 15 years
  • Habitat: Boreal forests of Siberia

Siberian Tigers are solitary hunters, primarily preying on large ungulates such as deer and wild boar. They are known for their powerful build and striking orange coat with black stripes, which provides excellent camouflage in their forested habitat. Despite their strength, Siberian Tigers are classified as endangered due to habitat loss and poaching.

African Elephant

African Elephants are the largest land animals on Earth.

  • Scientific Name: Loxodonta africana
  • Average Length: 24 ft
  • Average Lifespan: 60-70 years
  • Habitat: Savannas, forests, and grasslands

African Elephants are known for their intelligence and strong social bonds. They live in matriarchal herds led by the oldest female. These herds are made up of related females and their young, while adult males usually leave the herd when they reach maturity. African Elephants are herbivores, feeding on a variety of vegetation, including grasses, leaves, and fruits.
